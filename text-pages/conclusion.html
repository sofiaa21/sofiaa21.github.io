SF Crime Data 2007 — 2017 · Post-Crisis Analysis
Project Conclusion

Was there anything surprising or interesting in you found during the exploration?

We certainly agree that there are some neighbourhoods more dangerous than the other. The areas around Tenderloin and South of Market are the nest of the crime that starts to expand to Mission and Bayview over the years. Richmond stays the safest are throughout the period.

We can clearly see the increase of Property crime and the constant Violent crime in the city. We have seen that the rate of Sex offense is elevated but in comparison to other categories is not as significant. Drug crimes appear less but have stable high rates in Tenderloin and Bayview. Another interesting group of crimes that we can see in higher incidence are Administrative and Financial crimes located around Central district.

Reasons for people commiting crimes are complex. However, we can see that social and economic factors play a significant role in human behaviour. The areas with higher crime rate are also the ones with higher poverty, lower education, and higher unemployement. These areas were also facing these disadvantages before the Great Recession, which results that financial crisis further worsened their situation and made them more tend to find illegal ways to get what they need - money, housing, escape etc.

Key Insights
  • The financial crisis acted as a catalyst that "further worsened their situation" in already problematic districts (Tenderloin, Mission).
  • Money and housing acted as primary motivators for property crimes.
  • While crime remained concentrated in historically problematic areas the economic pressure of the era saw crime begin to expand into neighboring areas over the years following the crisis.
  • Sex Offenses had the steepest spike but due to large amount of changing policies, we can't confidently conclude it was caused by the Recession.
