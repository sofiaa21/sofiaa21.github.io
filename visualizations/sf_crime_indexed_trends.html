Chapter 02

Indexed Crime Trends

We pin point four major crime groups that we expect to be impacted by The Great Recession - Drugs & Alcohol, Property Crime, Violent Crime and Sex Offenses Based on human behavior theories and prior research, we hypothesize that with increased financial struggle, unemployement and collapsing housing market, crimes such as burglary or theft in general, will increase. To cope with stress, substance abuse is expected to rise and with it related offecnces - public or sexual violence.

↑ San Francisco crime incident data from 2007 to 2017 grouped by Crime Type. Index (100) shows relative change from 2007 baseline; the shaded red band marks the recession period. Hover over the lines to explore each category and year; index values above 100 indicate growth.

Understanding the Trends

Our findings showed a significant increase in Property Crime where index number in 2017 was around 50% higher and Sex Offences the index number has risen twice as high. Property Crime can be linked to collapse of the housing bubble - rise in homelessness and foreclosed properties offering targets for burglaries or squatting and drug abuse. The steepest increase is seen in the category of Sex offences - which includes prostitution, human trafficking and and can also be linked to homelesness and poverty, we are going to explore this in the next chapter.

Violent crime stays relatively stable and Drug & Alcohol decreased dramatically which does not support our first hypothesis saying that people get more violent or turn to substances when there is significant outside pressure.
